Education

2017-2020

When I first entered SMP Negeri 2 Sukoharjo, I felt a little awkward. The new environment, new friends, and a different atmosphere from elementary school made me have to adapt. However, over time, I started to enjoy my time there. One of the most memorable experiences was participating in extracurricular activities. 2017-2020

SMA Negeri 2 Pringsewu

After graduating from junior high school, I continued my education at Pringsewu 2 Public High School, a place where I further developed myself and discovered my interests and passions. In high school, the atmosphere is different—more challenging and more opportunities to explore different fields. From middle school to high school, I learned many things, such as the importance of friendship, hard work, and never giving up. Every challenge I face teaches me to always try to be better. Now that I am in college, I realize that all my experiences during school have shaped who I am today. I am grateful to have been part of SMP Negeri 2 Sukoharjo and SMA Negeri 2 Pringsewu, two schools that have provided many valuable memories and experiences in my life.

Now, I am a student in the Information Technology Study Program at UNY. The world of college is very different from school. Experience

Building the Foundation

My journey started at SMP Negeri 2 Sukoharjo, where I first encountered structured learning and social interactions that shaped my personality. During this period, I experienced many firsts—my first time joining extracurricular activities, participating in competitions, and working on group projects. The biggest challenge during this time was adjusting to new subjects like mathematics and science, which required a deeper understanding. But with the support of my teachers and friends, I learned that perseverance and a growth mindset are key to overcoming academic struggles.

Stepping Up the Game

Entering Pringsewu 2 Public High School is a very different and challenging experience. Academics became more demanding, but this is where I started to explore my true interests. At this school, I met friends who always supported and helped me in solving various challenges. They also often invite me to play volleyball, which is not only a physical activity, but also provides a valuable life lesson—that fun and happiness do not only come from personal achievements, but also from togetherness, cooperation, and simple moments shared with others. Another valuable experience was the university entrance exam preparation process. This journey taught me the importance of discipline, perseverance, and self-motivation. I have to be able to manage my time well, dividing my focus between intensive studying and various other school activities. Even though it was full of challenges, this experience shaped my character to be more independent and responsible, preparing me to face the wider world in the future.

Embracing New Challenges

Now, as a student of Information Technology at UNY, I face even bigger challenges but also greater opportunities. The transition from high school to university required me to be more independent and resourceful. I started learning advanced programming, software development, and digital transformation, which broadened my understanding of how technology shapes the world. Collaborating with diverse groups of students from different backgrounds helped me improve my communication and teamwork skills. University life also pushed me to develop time management skills, balancing lectures, assignments, and personal projects. One of my biggest experiences so far has been working on real-world projects and attending tech seminars that gave me insights into the industry. It has been both challenging and rewarding, and I am excited to see where this journey will take me.
